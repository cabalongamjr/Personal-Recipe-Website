Authentic Filipino Sinigang: A Tangy and Comforting Stew!

Delight in the tangy and savory flavors of Filipino Sinigang! This traditional stew features tender pork and a medley of vegetables simmered in a tamarind-based broth. Simple to make, incredibly delicious, and perfect for any meal. Try it and enjoy a taste of the Philippines!

Servings: 6-8

Preparation Time: 30 minutes

Cooking Time: 1-1.5 hours

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly or ribs, cut into chunks
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 2 large tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 radish, sliced
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 6-8 pieces okra, ends trimmed
  • 1 bunch water spinach (kangkong) or regular spinach
  • 1-2 green chili peppers
  • 1 pack tamarind soup base (about 40g)
  • 6 cups water
  • Fish sauce or salt, to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring 6 cups of water to a boil. Add the pork and simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the meat is tender.
  2. Add the onions and tomatoes, and cook for another 10 minutes until they are softened.
  3. Add the tamarind soup base and stir until it is fully dissolved.
  4. Lower the heat to a simmer and add the radish, eggplant, and okra. Cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender but not overcooked.
  5. Add the green chili peppers and spinach, and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  6. Season with fish sauce or salt to taste.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice. Enjoy!
